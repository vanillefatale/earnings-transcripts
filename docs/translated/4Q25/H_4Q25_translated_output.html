# Hyatt Hotels Earnings Call Summary

## Key Financial Metrics & Guidance

- **Net unit growth guidance**: 6-7% organic growth maintained, with strong pipeline momentum across new brands (Hyatt Select, Studios, Unscripted, UrCove)
- **Core fee growth**: Expected 7.5% at midpoint for 2026, with historical 8% CAGR since 2017
- **Cash flow conversion**: Targeting low-to-mid 50% EBITDA-to-free cash flow conversion in 2026
- **Capital returns**: $350M midpoint guidance, with plans for deleveraging to restore investment-grade ratios
- **Q4 2025 RevPAR**: Strong 4% global growth, though Q1 2026 guided to ~2% (midpoint of full-year range)

## Strategic Developments

- **AI investments**: Two years into comprehensive AI implementation with four large-scale agentic platforms live; seeing tangible results including 20% productivity gains in group sales and improved conversion rates
- **ALG Vacations impact**: ~$10M headwind in 2026 (primarily Q1) due to Jamaica hurricane damage and softer 4-star demand; management expects strong 2027 recovery with refreshed properties
- **Portfolio simplification**: Removing non-consolidated JV EBITDA from reporting; actively pursuing monetization of JV interests while retaining management contracts

## Management Tone & Outlook

**Confident and forward-looking**, with Mark Hoplamazian emphasizing "glass half full" momentum. Management stressed looking beyond near-term headwinds (Jamaica, inauguration comps) to structural strengths: 70% luxury/upper-upscale mix, strong international pipeline, and robust leisure demand. Defensive on short-term noise while highlighting long-term value creation.

## Key Risks & Considerations

- **Geographic concentration risk**: Significant Jamaica exposure creating 2026 volatility
- **Business transient weakness**: Flat-to-slightly-positive BT trends, though showing recent improvement
- **Execution complexity**: Multiple moving parts (AI rollout, brand launches, portfolio deals) require flawless execution to meet ambitious growth targets
