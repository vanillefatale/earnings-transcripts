# Intel Q4 Earnings Call Summary

## Key Financial Metrics & Guidance
- **Q1 Revenue Guidance**: $12.2B (below seasonal, down from Q4's $13.7B due to supply constraints)
- **Gross Margin**: Q1 at 34.5% (significantly pressured by supply constraints and Panther Lake dilution)
- **CapEx Strategy**: Flat to slightly down overall, but shifting from facility space to tooling investments
- **Custom ASIC Business**: Achieved $1B run rate with strong momentum

## Supply Constraints & Operational Challenges
- **Severe supply shortage** impacting both PC Client and Data Center segments; Q1 represents the "trough"
- Yield improvements of 7-8% per month being achieved, but not yet at industry-leading standards
- Finished goods inventory down 60% from peak levels, forcing hand-to-mouth operations
- Company prioritizing mid/high-end client products and data center customers over low-end PC
- Supply expected to improve through 2026, but constraints persist beyond Q1

## Management Tone & Strategic Priorities
- **Disciplined approach**: CEO Lip-Bu Tan refusing to deploy 14A capacity CapEx until customer commitments secured (expected H2 2026)
- Server roadmap simplified: focusing on 16-channel Diamond Rapids and accelerating Coral Rapids (reintroducing multi-threading)
- Strong hyperscaler engagement with long-term commitments; CPU demand for AI workloads exceeding expectations
- Advanced packaging (EMIB-T) emerging as major differentiator with deals exceeding $1B (vs. initial $100M expectations)

## Foundry Business Outlook
- **14A node**: Development on track, PDK 0.5 released, active customer test chip engagements
- Production timeline: Risk production late 2027, volume production 2028 (aligned with leading foundry competitors)
- Customer awards expected H2 2026; revenue materialization primarily 2027-2028
- Analyst Day planned for H2 2026 to provide detailed foundry strategy

## Key Risks
- Prolonged supply constraints may result in market share
